# Particle And Fixed Expression Rules

Use this file for Georgian particles, fixed expressions, conjunctions, conditionals, reported-speech suffixes, negation, and prohibition patterns.

## Fixed Expressions

Frequent expressions are not automatically written together. First identify their structure.

- `რა თქმა უნდა` remains three words because it is a transparent phrase, not one compounded word.
- `მხოლოდ და მხოლოდ` remains separate because `და` coordinates the repeated intensifier.
- `მაინცდამაინც` is lexicalized and written together.

When a fixed expression is transparent as a phrase, preserve the phrase. When it is lexicalized as a particle or adverb and no longer behaves as a phrase, joined writing may be standard.

## Source-Backed Particle Controls

Particles are helper words or bound elements that add semantic coloring to a word or to the whole sentence. Before deciding spacing, identify whether the element is:

- bound to the word: examples include suffix-like particles such as `-ც`, `-ვე`, `-ღა`, `-მე`, `-მეთქი`, `-თქო`, and reported-speech `-ო`
- separate before the word: `არ`, `ვერ`, `ნუ`
- separate or postposed by function: `ხომ`, `განა`, `ნუთუ`, `თურმე`, `ხოლმე`

Do not treat every frequent expression as a single word. First classify the grammatical relation.

## Conjunction Meaning

Use causal conjunctions for reason:

- `რადგან`
- `ვინაიდან`
- `იმიტომ, რომ`

Use purposive conjunctions for aim:

- `რათა`
- `იმისთვის, რომ`

Do not choose the conjunction by translating English "that" or "so that"; choose it from the Georgian clause relation.

Conjunctions are helper words that join sentence members or clauses. First decide whether the relation is coordinate or subordinate; then choose the conjunction.

## Conditional Clauses

Before choosing `თუ`, `რომ`, `როცა/როდესაც`, or `ოღონდ`, decide which relation the clause expresses.

- Real condition/result: use `თუ`, `რომ`, `როცა/როდესაც`, or `როგორც კი` when the condition can actually happen and the result follows from it.
- Explicit condition/result can use `თუ ... მაშინ ...`, but `მაშინ` is not always required in natural prose.
- Unreal or wished-for condition: use `რომ` with counterfactual or desired-state verb forms and a conditional result form.
- Request or bargain nuance: `ოღონდ` means "provided that/just do this" and should not be used as an ordinary substitute for `თუ`.

Do not translate English "if/when/as long as" mechanically. Georgian distinguishes real time, real condition, unreal condition, and request-like condition by construction and verb form.

## Relative Linkers

- Use `ვინც` or `ის, ვინც` for a person or actor.
- Use `რაც` or `ის, რაც` for a thing, event, content, or action-object relation.

Do not use `ვინც` only because English has "who" nearby; first identify whether the Georgian clause points to an acting person or to the content/object of an action.

## Reported-Speech Suffixes

Use bound reported-speech markers by speaker perspective:

- `-მეთქი`: the current speaker repeats the current speaker's own earlier words or thought.
- `-თქო`: the current speaker tells someone to pass on the current speaker's words.
- `-ო`: the current speaker reports someone else's words.

Attach these to the quoted utterance or its final word, and keep the person perspective inside the quote consistent. Treat them as discourse markers, not as standalone words.

## Negation Choice

- Use `ვერ` when an action cannot be done, failed, or did not happen because of inability, obstacle, or reason.
- Use `არ` for plain negation where the focus is not inability.
- Georgian negative pronouns and adverbs often pair with the finite negator: `არავინ არ`, `არაფერი არ`, `არსად არ`, `არასდროს არ`. Do not delete the second negative by English analogy.
- For prohibitions, still use the separate `არ`/`ნუ` rule below.

## Semantic Helper Words

Choose helper words by the relation they express:

- `თითქმის`: nearly/almost in amount, state, or completion.
- `კინაღამ`: a near-miss event that almost happened.
- `თითქოს`: apparent or "as if" action/state.
- `ნაცვლად`: substitution, "instead of"; do not treat it as a causal marker.
- `მოდი`: a movement verb in `მოდი ჩემთან`; a proposal helper with a comma in `მოდი, წავიდეთ`.

## Prohibition

Use `არ` with second-subjunctive style prohibitions:

- `არ უთხრა`
- `არ გააკეთო`

Use `ნუ` with present or future-style prohibitions:

- `ნუ ხსნი`
- `ნუ გახსნი`

## Answering Short Questions

If the user gives two spellings, do not choose by frequency or intuition. Classify the form:

- phrase with independent words: keep separate
- lexicalized particle/adverb: write together
- compound word with coordinate parts: consider hyphenation
