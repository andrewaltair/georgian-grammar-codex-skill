# Agreement Rules

Use this file for subject-predicate agreement, numeral phrases, relative-pronoun agreement, and polite `თქვენ`.

## Polite And Plural `თქვენ`

Keep `თქვენ` aligned with plural verb morphology, even when addressing one person politely.

- prefer: `თქვენ ხართ`
- prefer: `თქვენ იცით`
- prefer: `თქვენ შეგიძლიათ`
- avoid: `თქვენ ხარ`
- avoid: `თქვენ იცი`
- avoid: `თქვენ შეგიძლია`

Use polite lexical substitutions only where they sound natural; do not over-formalize simple sentences.

Source-backed control: `თქვენ` is both second-person plural and a polite form. In both uses it takes plural verb agreement.

## Numerals And Quantifiers

After a cardinal numeral above one, Georgian normally keeps the counted noun singular.

- prefer: `ორი წიგნი`
- avoid: `ორი წიგნები`

After `რამდენი`, keep the noun singular.

- prefer: `რამდენი ბავშვი`
- avoid: `რამდენი ბავშვები`

If the quantified phrase is the subject, the predicate is often singular too.

- prefer: `ორი კაცი მოვიდა`
- avoid in strict standard prose: `ორი კაცი მოვიდნენ`

When the quantified noun phrase is used in strict grammar QA, prefer the singular noun and singular predicate unless the context clearly requires a lexicalized plural or an explicitly plural interpretation.

## Collective Subjects

Collective subjects usually take singular predicate forms unless context strongly marks individual members.

Hyphenated coordinate compounds such as family-pair compounds can function as singular collective subjects; prefer singular predicate agreement in strict standard prose.

## Relative Pronouns

For `რომელიც`, decide both number and case before writing the form.

- Use singular `რომელიც` for one person, animal, thing, or event.
- Use plural `რომლებიც` for more than one referent, even when a numeral keeps the noun itself singular.
- The case of `რომელიც` is determined by its role inside the relative clause, not automatically by the case of the antecedent:
  - nominative role: `რომელიც`, `რომლებიც`
  - ergative role: `რომელმაც`, `რომლებმაც`
  - dative role: `რომელსაც`, `რომლებსაც`
  - genitive role: `რომლის`, formal plural `რომელთა`

If a collective noun such as `ხალხი`, `სტუდენტობა`, `ფარა`, or `ჯოგი` is treated as one group, singular agreement may be natural. If the sentence stresses individual members, re-evaluate plural agreement.

## Direct Address

In direct address, use vocative-like forms where standard Georgian requires them.

- reference: `ბატონი გიორგი მოვიდა`
- direct address: `ბატონო გიორგი, შემობრძანდით`
