# Adverb Rules

Use this file when checking Georgian adverbs (`ზმნიზედა`), adverbial relation, question words, relative adverbs, indefinite adverbs, negative adverbs, and adverb formation. Keep it as a classification guide; do not turn it into an adverb list.

## Core Rule

An adverb is an indeclinable part of speech. It usually modifies the action or state expressed by a verb and can mark place, time, manner, cause, purpose, frequency, negation, or a question/relative relation.

Do not force an adverb into noun case agreement. If the form behaves like a case-marked noun plus postposition, route to `case-and-postposition-rules.md`; if it behaves as a fixed helper word or particle, route to `particle-expression-rules.md`.

## Semantic Classes

Classify by the question the adverb answers:

- place: `სად?`, `საიდან?`, `საითკენ?`, `საით?`, `სადამდე?`
- time: `როდის?`, `როდიდან?`, `როდემდე?`, `რა დროიდან?`
- manner: `როგორ?`, `რანაირად?`
- cause: `რატომ?`
- purpose: `რისთვის?`
- frequency/count: `რამდენჯერ?`

Use the semantic class to choose the construction. Do not translate English adverbs mechanically when Georgian would prefer a postpositional phrase, subordinate clause, or different word order.

## Question, Relative, Indefinite, And Negative Adverbs

High-value patterns:

- question adverbs ask directly: `სად?`, `როდის?`, `რატომ?`
- relative adverbs can be formed from question adverbs plus `-ც`: `სადაც`, `როდესაც`
- indefinite adverbs can mark vague place/time: `სადღაც`, `სადმე`, `ოდესღაც`
- negative adverbs mark negated place/time: `არსად`, `ვერსად`, `არასდროს`

For negative adverbs, keep Georgian negation behavior in view. Forms such as `არსად` or `არასდროს` often still pair with a finite negator in the clause; do not delete the second negative by English analogy.

## Formation Checks

Georgian forms adverbs by several routes:

- primary adverbs: fixed forms not transparently built in the current sentence
- composition: repeated or compounded adverbial forms
- case markers: especially forms functioning adverbially rather than as ordinary noun phrases
- postpositions: noun/pronoun plus postposition used adverbially
- particles: attached or fixed elements that create adverbial meaning

Before judging spelling or spacing, classify the formation route:

1. If it is a transparent phrase, keep it separate.
2. If it is a lexicalized adverb, joined writing may be standard; use `compound-writing-rules.md` and `particle-expression-rules.md`.
3. If it is a postpositional expression, check the governing case.
4. If the standard written form is not derivable from the local rules, treat it as a lexical-norm case.

## Sentence Adverbs And Insertions

Some adverbs modify the verb directly; others comment on the whole sentence or act like parenthetical material. Do not decide punctuation from the word class alone.

1. If the adverb answers a normal action question such as `როგორ?`, `როდის?`, or `სად?`, treat it as an ordinary sentence member unless syntax says otherwise.
2. If the word expresses evaluation, certainty, speaker attitude, source, limitation, or transition for the whole sentence, route punctuation through `polite-forms-and-punctuation.md`.
3. In school-style punctuation tasks, sentence-initial evaluative or modal adverbs may be treated as inserted/non-member words and comma-separated.
4. If both readings are possible, classify the intended function from the sentence before choosing punctuation.

## Adverbs Versus Clause Relations

Some meanings can be expressed either by an adverb or by a clause:

- cause: adverbs such as `ამიტომ`, or clauses with `რადგან` / `იმიტომ, რომ`
- purpose: adverbial forms such as `ამისთვის`, or clauses with `რათა` / `იმისთვის, რომ`
- place/time: simple adverbs, postpositional phrases, or relative clauses with `სადაც` / `როდესაც`

Choose the structure that gives natural Georgian. For polished writing, avoid overloading one sentence with stacked adverbs when a shorter clause is clearer.

## Fast Checks

- If the user asks what type a word is, classify by question and function, not only by form.
- If an adverbial form contains `-ც`, check whether it is relative (`სადაც`) or additive/particle-like in context.
- If an adverb is negative, check the whole clause's negation pattern.
- If an adverb looks like a case-marked noun, confirm whether it is now lexicalized or still a normal noun phrase.
- If the issue is joined/separate spelling, route through orthography and compound rules before answering.
